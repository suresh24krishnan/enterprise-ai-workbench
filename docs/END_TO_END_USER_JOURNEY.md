# End-to-End User Journey — Enterprise AI Workbench

**Version:** Phase 1 — Reference Implementation (v1.0.0)
**Live Demo:** https://huggingface.co/spaces/sureshkrishnan/enterprise-ai-workbench

This document traces the complete user journey through the Enterprise AI Workbench — from login to immutable audit record — describing what the user does, what AI does, what governance does, what evidence is used, what is recorded in the audit trail, and what happens next at every step.

---

## Step 1 — Enterprise Login

### What the user does
The adjuster navigates to the platform and authenticates with their enterprise credentials. In Phase 1, authentication is mocked and the user is pre-authenticated as John Smith, Claims Adjuster.

### What AI does
Nothing. Login is a pure identity and access control operation.

### What governance does
Establishes the security context that will be passed to the governance engine on every subsequent AI request. The user's identity, role, and permissions are bound to the session.

### What evidence is used
None.

### What is recorded in the audit trail
**Event 1: `session.established`**
- Actor type: SYSTEM
- Status: INFO
- Description: User session established with identity and permissions
- Actor name: System Identity Service
- Timestamp: 2026-06-13 09:58:00 UTC

### What happens next
The user is redirected to the Executive Dashboard.

## Step 2 — Executive Dashboard

### What the user does
Leadership or the adjuster reviews the AI Operations Dashboard — KPIs, model utilization, governance distribution, platform health. This is an observation step; no AI actions are initiated here.

### What AI does
Nothing. The dashboard is a reporting surface populated from the analytics store (Phase 1: static mock data).

### What governance does
Nothing actively. The governance distribution panel shows the historical outcome of all governance evaluations this month: 96% ALLOW, 3% ESCALATE, 1% DENY, zero policy violations.

### What evidence is used
None.

### What is recorded in the audit trail
No audit event generated on the dashboard screen. Audit events are generated by AI actions on individual claims.

### What happens next
The adjuster navigates to the Claims Workbench to begin working a specific claim.

## Step 3 — Claims Workbench

### What the user does
The adjuster views their assigned claim queue. Claims are displayed with status, type, priority, and AI readiness indicators. The adjuster selects a claim to begin the AI-assisted workflow.

### What AI does
Nothing on the list view. AI analysis has been pre-computed for each claim (or is triggered on first access in Phase 2).

### What governance does
Claim list access is governed by the adjuster's authorised claim portfolio — the adjuster sees only claims they are permitted to access (Phase 2: enforced by ClaimCenter access control).

### What evidence is used
None at this stage.

### What is recorded in the audit trail
**Event 2: `claim.accessed`**
- Actor type: USER
- Status: INFO
- Description: Adjuster accessed claim record CLM-2026-100245
- Actor name: John Smith
- Timestamp: 2026-06-13 09:58:15 UTC

### What happens next
The adjuster opens the claim detail. The platform begins loading the claim context, triggering evidence retrieval and preparing for AI summarisation.

## Step 4 — AI Claim Summary

### What the user does
The adjuster views the AI-generated summary of the claim. The summary includes: coverage position, key facts, liability assessment, and recommended action. The adjuster reads the summary and reviews the confidence score (94% in this example) before deciding whether to proceed.

### What AI does

**Step 4a — Evidence retrieval (RAG)**
The knowledge provider (`IKnowledgeProvider`) retrieves the most relevant documents for this claim from the evidence store. Documents are ranked by semantic relevance. Retrieved evidence in this example includes:
- Police report (relevance: 0.94)
- Repair estimate from certified auto body shop (relevance: 0.91)
- Claimant statement — recorded interview (relevance: 0.88)
- Policy document — comprehensive coverage section (relevance: 0.85)
- Photos — vehicle damage (relevance: 0.79)
- Prior claim history (relevance: 0.61)

**Step 4b — Model routing**
The model router (`IModelRouter`) evaluates the task type (claim summarisation), risk level (medium — standard auto claim), and cost constraints. Selection: GPT-4.1-mini (52% of summarisation requests — routine task).

**Step 4c — AI generation**
The model provider (`IModelProvider`) invokes the selected model with the claim context and retrieved evidence. The model produces a structured summary grounded exclusively in the retrieved evidence documents. Confidence score: 94%.

### What governance does

**Before evidence retrieval:** governance engine evaluates whether this adjuster is authorised to retrieve evidence for this claim. Outcome: ALLOW.

**Before model invocation:** governance engine evaluates the summarisation request against `mvp_policy_set v1.0`. Checks: adjuster authorised for this claim? (yes), claim in active status? (yes), task type within allowed operations? (yes). Outcome: ALLOW. Latency: 12ms.

If governance had returned DENY, the summary would not be generated. The DENY reason would be displayed to the adjuster and recorded in the audit trail.

### What evidence is used
All six retrieved evidence documents listed above. The AI summary cites specific documents for each conclusion (e.g., "Repair cost estimate: $12,400 — source: Certified Auto Body Repair Estimate, confidence 0.91").

### What is recorded in the audit trail

**Event 3: `evidence.retrieved`**
- Actor type: AI
- Status: SUCCESS
- Description: 6 evidence documents retrieved and ranked for claim CLM-2026-100245
- Evidence count: 6
- Timestamp: 2026-06-13 09:58:31 UTC

**Event 4: `governance.evaluated`**
- Actor type: GOVERNANCE
- Status: ALLOW
- Description: Policy evaluation completed — summarisation request approved
- Policy set: mvp_policy_set v1.0
- Latency: 12ms
- Timestamp: 2026-06-13 09:58:44 UTC

**Event 5: `model.routed`**
- Actor type: AI
- Status: SUCCESS
- Description: Model router selected GPT-4.1-mini for summarisation task
- Routing reason: Standard summarisation — cost-optimised routing
- Timestamp: 2026-06-13 09:58:47 UTC

**Event 6: `ai.summary.generated`**
- Actor type: AI
- Status: SUCCESS
- Description: AI claim summary generated with 6 evidence sources
- Confidence: 94%
- Latency: 847ms

### What happens next
The adjuster proceeds to the AI Claim Assistant to ask specific questions about the claim, or navigates directly to Draft Note if the summary is sufficient.

## Step 5 — AI Claim Assistant

### What the user does
The adjuster asks targeted questions about the claim. Example:

> "Is the repair estimate within policy limits for this coverage type?"

> "Are there any coverage exclusions that apply to this incident?"

> "What does the claimant statement say about the circumstances of the incident?"

The adjuster can ask multiple questions in a single session. Each question-answer pair is governed and audited independently.

### What AI does
For each question:

1. The conversation service appends the question to the conversation history.
2. The knowledge provider retrieves evidence relevant to the specific question.
3. The model provider generates a response using: the claim context, the retrieved evidence, and the conversation history.
4. The response is grounded exclusively in the retrieved evidence — the AI cannot introduce information from outside the claim context.
5. Citations are provided with each response.

Context isolation is enforced by the orchestration service: the AI's context window contains only the retrieved evidence for this specific claim. The model has no access to other claims.

### What governance does
**Per-turn governance evaluation:** every question submitted by the adjuster triggers a governance check before the AI responds.

Checks include:
- Is the adjuster authorised to ask AI questions about this claim? (yes)
- Is the question within the permitted scope? (claim-related questions: yes; out-of-scope queries: DENY)
- Is the conversation within session policy? (session active and not expired: yes)

Out-of-scope query example: if the adjuster asks "What is the weather forecast for tomorrow?", governance returns DENY with reason: "Query is outside the authorised scope for this claim session." The AI does not respond. The DENY is recorded in the audit trail.

### What evidence is used
Evidence is retrieved per-question — the knowledge provider finds the most relevant documents for the specific question asked, not just the full claim evidence set.

### What is recorded in the audit trail

**Event 7: `conversation.turn`** (one per question-answer pair)
- Actor type: AI
- Status: SUCCESS
- Description: Adjuster question processed — response generated from 3 evidence sources
- Confidence: 91%
- Latency: 623ms
- Timestamp: 2026-06-13 10:00:02 UTC

**Event 8: `governance.evaluated`** (one per turn)
- Actor type: GOVERNANCE
- Status: ALLOW
- Description: Per-turn governance check — conversation request approved
- Policy set: mvp_policy_set v1.0
- Latency: 9ms
- Timestamp: 2026-06-13 10:00:01 UTC

### What happens next
The adjuster, satisfied with the AI analysis, proceeds to review the evidence panel and then request the AI draft note.

## Step 6 — Evidence & Explainability

### What the user does
The adjuster reviews the evidence panel. Each document retrieved for the claim is displayed with its relevance score and content excerpt. The adjuster can verify:
- Which documents the AI used to generate the summary
- The relevance ranking of each document
- Whether any critical documents are missing

This step is optional but strongly recommended before approving the draft note — it confirms that the AI was working from the right evidence.

### What AI does
No new AI execution on this screen. The evidence displayed was retrieved in Step 4 (evidence retrieval). The adjuster is reviewing the AI's source material, not triggering new AI actions.

### What governance does
Nothing new on this screen. The evidence retrieval was governed at Step 4.

### What evidence is used
All evidence retrieved in Step 4, displayed with full metadata: document type, relevance score, content excerpt, retrieval timestamp, and whether each document was used in the AI summary.

### What is recorded in the audit trail
No new audit event generated by viewing the evidence panel. The evidence retrieval event (Event 3) covers this step.

### What happens next
The adjuster proceeds to the Draft Note screen.

## Step 7 — AI Draft Claim Note

### What the user does
The adjuster views the AI-generated draft note. The note is structured in the standard claims adjuster note format and includes: claim overview, coverage assessment, key findings from the evidence, recommended action, and adjuster attribution. The note is displayed with a DRAFT watermark.

The adjuster reads the note carefully. They may:
- Accept it as written and proceed to approval
- Note specific sections to verify against the evidence (Phase 2: inline editing)

### What AI does
The model provider generates the draft note using:
- The AI claim summary from Step 4
- The retrieved evidence documents
- The adjuster conversation from Step 5 (if any questions were asked)
- The note template from `shared/prompts/`

The note is grounded in the same evidence used for the summary. It does not introduce new conclusions. It translates the analysis into the standard adjuster note format.

### What governance does
The note generation request is evaluated by the governance engine before the model is invoked. The governance check confirms: is the adjuster authorised to generate a note for this claim? Is the claim in a state that permits note generation (active, not closed)? Outcome: ALLOW.

The governance evaluation is recorded before the note is generated.

### What evidence is used
The same evidence set from Step 4, plus the summary generated in Step 4 and any conversation insights from Step 5.

### What is recorded in the audit trail

**Event 9: `draft.note.generated`**
- Actor type: AI
- Status: SUCCESS
- Description: Draft adjuster note generated — held in DRAFT status pending human approval
- Confidence: 93%
- Latency: 1,124ms
- Timestamp: 2026-06-13 10:01:47 UTC

### What happens next
The adjuster clicks "Review and Approve" to open the Governed Approval screen.

## Step 8 — Governed Approval

### What the user does
The adjuster completes the structured approval process. This is the human decision gate — the architectural enforcement of human authority over AI.

The adjuster:
1. Reviews the draft note in full
2. Completes the approval checklist:
   - [ ] I have reviewed the AI claim summary
   - [ ] I have verified the evidence citations
   - [ ] I confirm the coverage assessment is accurate
   - [ ] I confirm the recommended action is appropriate
   - [ ] I accept responsibility for this claim decision
3. Optionally adds a comment or note
4. Clicks "Approve and Submit" — explicitly committing their identity to the decision

The adjuster can also click "Reject" — in which case the draft note is returned to the draft stage, the rejection reason is recorded, and the workflow may restart from Step 7 or Step 4.

### What AI does
Nothing. The approval screen is a human decision surface. The AI has no role in the approval decision.

### What governance does
The approval submission is validated by the governance engine:
- All checklist items must be confirmed before the approval can be submitted
- The submitter identity must match the authenticated adjuster for this session — another user cannot approve on behalf of this adjuster
- If the claim has been escalated (governance returned ESCALATE at any prior step), a supervisor approval is also required (Phase 2)

### What evidence is used
The draft note, the AI summary, and the evidence citations — all presented to the adjuster on this screen for final review.

### What is recorded in the audit trail

**Event 10: `approval.submitted`**
- Actor type: USER
- Status: SUCCESS
- Description: Adjuster reviewed and approved the AI draft note — all checklist items confirmed
- Actor name: John Smith
- Timestamp: 2026-06-13 10:03:22 UTC

If rejected:
- Status: DENY
- Description: Adjuster rejected the AI draft note — [rejection reason]

### What happens next
The approval record triggers the write-back sequence.

## Step 9 — Secure Write / Write Confirmation

### What the user does
The adjuster sees the write confirmation screen — confirmation that the approval was recorded and the note is flagged as write-ready.

In Phase 1: the system records "Ready for Write-back" — the note is approved but the actual ClaimCenter API call is not implemented.

In Phase 2: the `IClaimNoteWriter` adapter calls the ClaimCenter sandbox or production API with the approved note, receives a confirmation reference, and displays it to the adjuster.

### What AI does
Nothing. Write-back is a governed system action following human approval.

### What governance does
Write-back only proceeds when a valid, unexpired approval record exists for the note. The governance engine confirms the approval record integrity before the write-back adapter is invoked. If no valid approval exists, the write is blocked.

### What evidence is used
The approved note content and the approval record from Step 8.

### What is recorded in the audit trail

**Event 11: `claimcenter.note.written`**
- Actor type: SYSTEM
- Status: SUCCESS
- Description: The approved draft note is ready for governed write-back
- Timestamp: 2026-06-13 10:04:05 UTC

In Phase 2, the event description will be updated to:
- "Note written to ClaimCenter — reference [ClaimCenter-issued ID]"

### What happens next
The adjuster navigates to the Audit Trail to review the complete record of the workflow.

Each event card is expandable to show full metadata: actor type, actor name, status, description, evidence count, confidence, policy set, routing reason, latency, and timestamp.

The right panel shows:
- Summary metrics: total events, AI events, governance events, human events, ALLOW count, evidence retrievals, total latency, average confidence
- 10-step execution path with completion indicators for each step

### What AI does
Nothing. The audit screen is a read-only display of the immutable event record.

### What governance does
Nothing new. The audit record displays the governance outcomes that were recorded at each step.

### What evidence is used
The full audit event stream from `IAuditStore`. In Phase 1, this is an in-memory store. In Phase 2, it is a PostgreSQL database with a write-only audit user.

## Key Properties of the Governed Workflow

**1. AI never acts without governance clearance.**
Every AI action is preceded by a governance evaluation. The model is never invoked without an ALLOW decision. DENY blocks the action. ESCALATE routes to a human reviewer.

**2. AI never writes to a system of record without human approval.**
The Governed Approval gate (Step 8) is enforced in the service layer — it is not a UI suggestion. The `IClaimNoteWriter` adapter is only invoked after a valid approval record exists.

**3. Every AI output is grounded in cited evidence.**
The AI operates exclusively within the retrieved evidence context. It cannot introduce information from outside the claim documents. Citations are provided with every output.

**4. The audit trail is the single source of truth.**
Every significant action — AI and human — is recorded in the audit trail with actor type, status, confidence, and latency. The record is append-only and cannot be altered.

**5. The workflow is deterministic and repeatable.**
Given the same claim and evidence, the workflow produces the same governance outcomes, the same audit events, and the same approval gate. There are no unrecorded shortcuts.
